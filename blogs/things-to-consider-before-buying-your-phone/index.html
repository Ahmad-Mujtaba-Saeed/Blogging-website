Things to consider before buying your phone

  • Tokyo Doe
  • 26 April 2022
  • 3
  • 562
Tweet Pin Email

It’s important to keep certain 6 things in mind before buying a new smartphone, this is so you can make the best decision and maximize your user experience. Smartphones have, over time, become a sort of indispensable companion, especially because of their use in helping to stay on top of important email and social updates. With this, it’s safe to say that choosing the right smartphone for yourself is indeed a big deal.

1. The Operating System:

When your budget for a new smartphone allows you to purchase either an iPhone with Apple iOS or a smartphone with the Android operating system, the decision becomes which of the two to purchase. Some argue that Apple's iOS is superior to Google's Android OS, but the truth is that it depends.

The Apple iOS (Operating System) on all iPhones is simple to use, and you can expect to get the newest and best apps first, as well as timely software upgrades, strict security, and very rare virus issues. However, with the Android OS, you have more hardware options and greater control over your user experience.

2. The Camera:

For the majority of smartphone buyers, this is without a doubt the most significant feature. Consider factors like megapixels (the higher the better), aperture (the lower the better), and, if it's within your budget, try to acquire one with optical image stabilisation when purchasing a smartphone (OIS). It's okay if you can't find one with OIS; instead, focus mostly on the camera's megapixels and aperture.



3. The Ram And Processor:

Consider both the RAM and processor of the smartphone for better CPU performance, faster processes, and better multitasking on your smartphone. A good RAM and processor, for example, will lessen, if not fully eliminate, the occurrence of your smartphone's 'hanging' while in use. However, RAM is now a more trustworthy measure of a phone's performance than the CPU, because a dual-core processor can be significantly more powerful than an eight-core processor in some cases.

Smartphones with 2GB or more RAM are recommended. Smartphones with insufficient RAM are the source of rudimentary performance issues. 1GB and 1.5GB RAM can be relatively limited, causing performance issues on your smartphone. However, if 1GB and 1.5GB RAM smartphones are within your price range, you should consider them.



4. Battery Life:

When purchasing a new smartphone, ensure sure the battery life is enough. If you value endurance, smartphones with 3000mAh batteries are usually suggested. You can choose one with a capacity greater than 2000mAh, while one with a capacity less than 2000mAh should be avoided.



5. Storage Options :

Most cellphones have inbuilt storage that ranges from 4GB to 64GB. When purchasing a smartphone, it is typically advised to choose one with 32GB of internal storage because, after system partitions, the user-accessible storage can be as low as 16GB. If a 32GB smartphone is out of your price range, you can settle for an 16GB model and add an external storage option like a microSD memory card.



6. The Screen Size:

If one-handed use is crucial to you and you're seeking for a portable smartphone you can use for fast chores, you should look for smartphones with screens less than 5 inches. A smartphone with a large screen size (5 inches and above) is better for you if, however, you want to watch films, play video games, browse the internet (particularly for reading text on a website), or otherwise use your phone mostly for media rather than rapid chores.
